These games are analogy to chemical kinetics and designed to easily understand the mechanisms
  • Instead of actual molecules, we have one container which contains equal-sized balls, A and B, and stores information as colors (A = blue, B = red)
  • We put our hand into the container and draw ball(s) blindly and successively. Every selected ball is identified visually as an A-ball or a B-ball
  • When a reaction happens, a drawn ball is replaced by ball of the opposite color taken from stock
  • A, B, X: represent the initial numbers of each ball
  • Time: represetnts how many times you draw ball(s) from a box
  • Probability: represents a probability that a reaction will occur after drawing ball(s)
  • Repeats: represents how many times you repeat a simulation
