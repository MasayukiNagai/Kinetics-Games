These games are analogy to chemical kinetics and designed to easily understand the mechanisms
  • Instead of actual molecules, we have one container which contains equal-sized balls, A and B, and stores information as colors (A = blue, B = red).
  • We put our hand into the container and draw ball(s) blindly and successively. Every selected ball is identified visually as an A-ball or a B-bal.l
  • When a reaction happens, a drawn ball is replaced by a ball of the opposite color taken from stock.
  • In addition to A and B, X-balls can be added. They represent other molecules, such as solvent, that are not involved with the reaction. Thus, adding more X-balls means the less concentrated solution.
  • In the "Games" tabs, you see the following paramters in the sidepanel:
    • Type of Reaction: A reaction mechanism you want to simulate
    • A, B, X: The initial numbers of each ball
    • Time: The number of times you draw ball(s) from a box
    • Probability: A probability that the reaction will occur after drawing a ball
    • Repeats: The number of times you repeat the whole simulation
